In a lively kingdom where laughter filled the air, joy was the essence of life. One day, King Grim, annoyed by the constant cheer, decided to tax laughter. With the new "Joke Tax," every jest and giggle came at a cost. Fearful of the penalties, the once jovial citizens stopped telling jokes. The kingdom, once alive with mirth, grew silent. The vibrant streets now echoed with an eerie quiet, as the spirit of laughter was stifled by the heavy hand of the king's decree.
